Lead Quality Architecture — Four Pillars I Enforce From Day 1
Consent Integrity
TrustedForm or Jornaya on every O&O form. Active-consent handshake before lead enters the ping-post flow. TCPA exposure eliminated at source.
Contact Validity
Phone validation (Trestle) + RRDB Live Contactability filters. No disconnected numbers, no low-probability contacts delivered to buyers.
Intent Qualification
Progressive disclosure forms with qualifying questions downstream. Higher intent signals = higher marketplace bid price = wider margin spread.
Source Accountability
Sub-source CPL floor enforcement. Any publisher at 2x floor or below 40% contact rate is paused. No exceptions. Quality is non-negotiable.

Resource Allocation Framework
💰
Steps Toward Vertical Resource Allocation — Improving Agency P&L
How I prioritize team time, budget, and attention across verticals to maximize margin contribution

Resource allocation is not set at hire. It is earned by data. The process below governs how team bandwidth, media budget, and strategic attention get distributed across verticals and agency clients to maximize P&L contribution at the portfolio level.

1
Score Every Vertical
By Day 30, each vertical gets a P&L score: current margin per lead, marketplace bid price, volume capacity, and compliance complexity. This is the allocation starting point, not a gut call.
Output: Vertical P&L scorecard shared with leadership.
2
Tier by Margin Potential
Verticals are ranked: Tier 1 (highest margin spread, highest buyer demand), Tier 2 (growth potential, needs investment), Tier 3 (maintenance mode, low margin). Resources follow the tiers.
Output: Tiered vertical map with recommended team ownership per tier.
3
Allocate Team Bandwidth
Senior talent goes to Tier 1 verticals. Tier 2 gets a dedicated growth owner. Tier 3 is process-managed with automated reporting and a monthly review. No one is spread across all four verticals equally.
Output: Team ownership map with clear vertical accountability.
4
Rebalance Quarterly
At each QBR, the vertical scorecard is refreshed. If a Tier 2 vertical has closed the margin gap, it earns more resources. If a Tier 1 vertical is plateauing, resources shift to the next growth opportunity.
Output: Quarterly reallocation recommendation presented to leadership.

KPI Master Stack — What I Track & Why
O&O: Margin Per Lead Primary P&L metric. Lead sale price minus media cost, by vertical and source. The scorecard.
O&O: Form Completion Rate % of sessions reaching confirmed submit, by device. Most direct lever on margin.
O&O: Contact Rate by Source % of delivered leads that are reachable. Below 40% = source gets paused.
O&O: Lead-to-Sale Lag Days from submission to downstream conversion. Sets CPL floor accuracy.
Agency: Client Efficiency Score CPL vs. benchmark, ROAS trend, YoY growth. Tells me if the relationship is defensible.
Paid Search: Impression Share Lost Lost IS by budget vs. rank — tells me where to fight for volume vs. quality.
Paid Social: Hook Rate + Hold Rate 3-sec views / impressions and 50% watch / 3-sec views. Diagnoses creative fatigue.
SEO: Keyword Velocity New top-10 rankings per month by intent tier. Measures organic momentum.
Team: Test Velocity Statistically valid tests completed per month. Measures learning capacity, not just execution.

Priority First Tests — Days 31–60
  • 1
    O&O Form: Progressive Disclosure. 3-field vs. 5-field vs. progressive multi-step. Hypothesis: fewer initial fields raises form start rate; progressive flow raises lead quality score and marketplace bid price.
  • 2
    Paid Search: Match Type Restructure. Collapse broad-modified into exact/phrase with tighter negatives. Hypothesis: CPL drops 15–25% within 30 days by eliminating junk query traffic.
  • 3
    Meta: Creative Format Isolation. Static vs. UGC-style video vs. testimonial carousel on top O&O vertical. Format variable isolated; audience and copy held constant.
  • 4
    Sub-source Prune. Cut any vendor at 2x CPL floor or below 40% contact rate. Immediate margin recapture with zero new spend. First visible win for leadership.
  • 5
    Mobile CTA: Sticky Bar vs. Inline vs. Tap-to-Call. Top O&O funnel on mobile. Mobile CVR gap is almost always the first margin unlock on any owned funnel.
